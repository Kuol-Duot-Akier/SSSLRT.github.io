Welcome to SSSLR-T

South Sudan Sign Language Recognition and Translation (SSSLRT) is an AI-powered system designed to recognize and translate sign language used by the deaf community in South Sudan.

"Bridging the communication gap between the deaf and hearing communities in South Sudan through artificial intelligence."

About

South Sudanese Sign Language lacks technological support, especially in healthcare where communication barriers can be life-threatening.

  • Recognizes common health-related signs (e.g., "malaria," "diarrhea," "sick").
  • Translates into readable English text.
  • Operates in real-time with minimal latency.

Objectives

  • Empower the deaf community in clinical settings.
  • Leverage AI for social good.
  • Lay the groundwork for a national sign language dataset.

Dataset

  • Total Classes: 7 health-related words
  • Samples per Class: ~20 videos
  • Video Length: ~2 seconds
  • Frame Rate: 30 frames per video
  • Features: 1662 keypoints per frame using MediaPipe Holistic
  • Shape: (num_samples, 30, 1662)

Model

Our system uses a Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) network to process video frame sequences.

Input Shape: (30 frames, 1662 features)
Layers:
- LSTM (64 units)
- LSTM (128 units)
- LSTM (64 units)
- Dense (64) + ReLU
- Dense (32) + ReLU
- Dense (7) + Softmax

Training: 163 epochs, Adam optimizer, Categorical Crossentropy loss, built in TensorFlow/Keras.

Results

  • Training Accuracy: ~98%
  • Validation Accuracy: ~92%
  • Latency: Less than 0.5s per prediction
